Singapore,[f] officially the Republic of Singapore, is an island country and city-state in Southeast Asia. The country's territory comprises one main island, 63 satellite islands and islets, and one outlying islet. It is about one degree of latitude (137 kilometres or 85 miles) north of the equator, off the southern tip of the Malay Peninsula, bordering the Strait of Malacca to the west, the Singapore Strait to the south along with the Riau Islands in Indonesia, the South China Sea to the east, and the Straits of Johor along with the State of Johor in Malaysia to the north.In its early history, Singapore was a maritime emporium known as Temasek; subsequently, it was part of a major constituent part of several successive thalassocratic empires. Its contemporary era began in 1819, when Stamford Raffles established Singapore as an entrep√¥t trading post of the British Empire. In 1867, Singapore came under the direct control of Britain as part of the Straits Settlements. During World War II, Singapore was occupied by Japan in 1942 and returned to British control as a Crown colony following Japan's surrender in 1945. Singapore gained self-governance in 1959 and, in 1963, became part of the new federation of Malaysia, alongside Malaya, North Borneo, and Sarawak. Ideological differences led to Singapore's expulsion from the federation two years later; Singapore became an independent sovereign country in 1965. After early years of turbulence and despite lacking natural resources and a hinterland, the nation rapidly developed to become one of the Four Asian Tigers.

Visiting Singapore

What to do in singapore
To travel to Singapore, you must submit an SG Arrival Card (SGAC) online no more than three days before your arrival, unless you're a resident or in transit without immigration clearance. You don't need a visa for tourist or business visits up to 90 days, but your passport must have at least six months' validity. Singapore has excellent public transport via the Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) system, and you can use credit/debit cards for payment. Prepare for tropical, hot, and humid weather with frequent rain, so pack light clothing and an umbrella. Health risks include Dengue fever and Zika virus, and you should use insect repellent and consider vaccination against Japanese Encephalitis. Iconic Landmarks: Visit Gardens by the Bay, with its Supertree Grove and light show. Museums: Explore the ArtScience Museum, the National Museum of Singapore, and the Battlebox Museum. Food: Experience Singapore's diverse culinary scene at its world-famous hawker centers.

Features

Iconic modern architectural landmarks:

  1. Gardens by the bay - A futuristic park featuring the Supertree Grove and massive conservatories like the Cloud Forest.
  2. Marina Bay Sands - An iconic luxury hotel with an observation deck, casino, and the ArtScience Museum located nearby
  3. Singapore Botanic Gardens - The country's first UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcasing vast collections of flora, including the National Orchid Garden.
  4. Night Safari - The world's first nocturnal zoo, allowing visitors to observe animals in their nighttime habitats.
